POEMS


Who am I?

Has she ever come to you,
that lady in green and grey,...

Who am I?
Has she ever come to you,
that lady in green and grey,
where I stood wearing those bold colors
and I change them right away
after a talk, a talk with any.

What originality do I possess,
If right after a speech
I’m changed, a whole new person

Call it progress,
Someone who doesn’t live with the past
Or is it
A person with no thoughts?

Today I wear purple, jet black shoes
Tomorrow a bright red ribbon on my hair
is there anything different that I will choose?

what are these colours
that I don’t see
these clocks
that wears me greyer after each tic

is it the night that I’m meant to be
is it its serenity or the loneliness?
or the day, also a possibility
a lovely sunny beach perhaps,
or
a working man’s sweat, in a job
not so befitting
that he curses me daily

who am I meant to be?

Is there a place I long to see,
Where I can stay,
Wear the same clothes everyday

Or this inevitable nature
That will keep me changing from day to day
Drowning self to severe pain
With constant fear of rapid change

Handing me no certainty
Of what colors I am to be

I, stand before you today
With an introduction that deserves
A meaning from the one who made me.

Where am I?

Seeing someone else live exactly as I wanted
Between the books of physics and those..

Where am I?
Seeing someone else live exactly as I wanted
Between the books of physics and those of poems I wrote
And love that by now should have surrounded me
I know my expectations aren’t par the sky
I see someone live the same dream, each day,
The dream that once belonged to me

bashing my head back and forth for being in a state
That is fulfilling to the world
I do realize I’m in a place that is someone’s dream
But mine
With questions around if I still have a chance to quit
Quit the world many give their souls to stay in
in my rebellious young mind, you will soon realize
the decisions we make for you are for your good
when the world wants to be where I am
maybe I am still young to be grateful for it
undermining my needs of just being able to fulfill
the dreams that I’ve lived in my head,
a Harshita painting the canvases with love and oils
I know my expectations aren’t par the sky
I see someone live the same dream, each day,
The dream that once belonged to me
