Who am I?

Has she ever come to you,
that lady in green and grey,...

Who am I?
Has she ever come to you,
that lady in green and grey,
where I stood wearing those bold colors
and I change them right away
after a talk, a talk with any.

What originality do I possess,
If right after a speech
I’m changed, a whole new person

Call it progress,
Someone who doesn’t live with the past
Or is it
A person with no thoughts?

Today I wear purple, jet black shoes
Tomorrow a bright red ribbon on my hair
is there anything different that I will choose?

what are these colours
that I don’t see
these clocks
that wears me greyer after each tic

is it the night that I’m meant to be
is it its serenity or the loneliness?
or the day, also a possibility
a lovely sunny beach perhaps,
or
a working man’s sweat, in a job
not so befitting
that he curses me daily

who am I meant to be?

Is there a place I long to see,
Where I can stay,
Wear the same clothes everyday

Or this inevitable nature
That will keep me changing from day to day
Drowning self to severe pain
With constant fear of rapid change

Handing me no certainty
Of what colors I am to be

I, stand before you today
With an introduction that deserves
A meaning from the one who made me.

Airplanes

God is sending me airplanes.
Airplanes that I don’t plan to use…

Airplanes
God is sending me airplanes.

Airplanes that I don’t plan to use…it hits me to have things that are of no good to me…the god… he sent me planes made of gold, silver, platinum….and even diamond that Tuesday.

I told him I don’t deserve this. I told him I didn’t need them. I told him that the ground is where I belong… the ground is where I carved my name…I was not filled with self-hate…and I don't underestimate, I was tired of flying in an airplane that even the shortest ride now would feel like a trip to the dark days.

I had wings I never used. Wings that I never appreciated. Wings that deserved love and wings that demanded more of. But they were wings that I never used. And as they grew heavy, I told myself that soaring was not my job. I was afraid of the dark colored pain. So I told self to stop hating the ground and that the sky was never meant for me. That, was the greatest mistake that I had ever made.

God was sending me airplanes I never used. I wish I had soared earlier. I wish I had flown higher. I wish I had loved them with all my heart into. i wish i was not filled with regret, the same regret that keeps me close to writing, to what i name growing, or healing, or a replacement of regret. i am happy to regret, i am happy that i regret.
